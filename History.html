Brajrajnagar: A Historical Timeline

Tracing the Evolution of a Town

  • 1936

    Establishment of Brajrajnagar Police Station and Orient Paper Mills

    In 1936, the foundations of Brajrajnagar were laid with the establishment of the Brajrajnagar Police Station. Concurrently, the renowned Orient Paper Mills, founded by the Birlas, took root in this locale. Dulichand Hanuman Prasad Shah assumed responsibility for managing the burgeoning township that sprouted around the mill, primarily inhabited by its workforce.

  • 1939

    Commencement of Orient Paper Mills Operations

    The year 1939 marked a significant milestone as the Orient Paper Mills commenced operations. This momentous event catalyzed the economic and social growth of the region. The township flourished, emerging as a central hub for the mill's employees and their families, shaping the town's identity and prosperity.

  • 1968

    Transition to Nagar Panchayat Status

    In 1968, Brajrajnagar transitioned to a Nagar Panchayat (Notified Area Council), reflecting its evolution into a local urban governing body. This pivotal administrative shift symbolized the town's increasing significance and the burgeoning population within its bounds.

  • 1989

    Elevation to Nagar Palika (Municipality) Status

    A landmark development occurred in 1989 when Brajrajnagar attained the esteemed status of a Nagar Palika (Municipality). Its jurisdiction expanded to encompass an area spanning 42.73 km2, encompassing seven revenue villages: Lamtibahal, Telenpali, Remja, Lajkura, Gandghora, Katabaga, and Sanjob, falling under its municipal governance
